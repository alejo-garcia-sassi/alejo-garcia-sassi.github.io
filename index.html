About Me

I am a mathematician with broad interests in Dynamical Systems, Low-dimensional Topology and Geometry, and Ergodic Theory, as well as their applications to areas such as Fluid Mechanics and Astrodynamics.

I currently hold a position as a Postdoctoral Researcher (FAPESP) at the Institute of Mathematics and Statistics, University of São Paulo (IME-USP). I am also a Junior Professor (on leave) at the Instituto de Matemática y Estadística Rafael Laguardia (IMERL), Universidad de la República (UdelaR).

I earned a joint PhD between USP and UdelaR in 2024, under the supervision of Alejandro Passeggi and Fábio Armando Tal. During my doctoral studies, I completed three research stays at Sorbonne Université (IMJ-PRG), where I worked with Pierre-Antoine Guihéneuf.

I am strongly involved in mathematical outreach. As a founding member of Talleres Matemática UdelaR, I have organized workshops and science-communication activities, and contributed to exhibitions, interviews, and columns.
